Skill Guide

Planche.

The ultimate test of pushing strength. A full planche requires extraordinary anterior deltoid, chest, and core activation — it's the crown jewel of calisthenics.

About This Skill

What is the Planche?

The planche is a gymnastics and calisthenics hold where the entire body is held horizontal and off the ground, supported only by the hands. It looks like levitation — and requires years of dedicated training to achieve.

The movement primarily targets the anterior deltoids, chest, and core, while also demanding significant wrist strength and body tension from head to toe.

Most athletes spend 1–3 years progressing from a tuck planche to a full planche hold. Patience and progressive overload are essential.

Progression

Your Planche Ladder.

Master each level before moving to the next. Consistency beats intensity.

1

Tuck Planche

Beginner

Knees tucked tight to chest. Focus on leaning forward with straight arms and protracted shoulders. Hold for 10–30 seconds.

Est. time: 2–4 months

2

Advanced Tuck Planche

Intermediate

Extend hips so back is flat and horizontal. Knees still bent but hips open. This is where real shoulder strength develops.

Est. time: 3–6 months

3

Straddle Planche

Advanced

Legs extended and spread wide. The wider the straddle, the easier. Gradually close the legs as strength develops.

Est. time: 6–12 months

4

Full Planche

Elite

Legs together, body perfectly horizontal with straight arms. The ultimate expression of calisthenics pushing strength. Few ever reach this stage.

Est. time: 12–36+ months
