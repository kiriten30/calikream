About This Skill

What are Handstand Push-ups?

The handstand push-up (HSPU) is an advanced calisthenics movement where you press your entire bodyweight overhead from an inverted position. It's the bodyweight equivalent of a strict overhead press — but significantly harder.

This movement primarily develops the shoulders, triceps, and upper back, while demanding significant balance and body awareness.

Progression

Your HSPU Ladder.

Build overhead pressing strength step by step.

1

Pike Push-ups

Beginner

Elevated pike position to build initial overhead pressing strength and shoulder stability.

Est. time: 1–3 months

2

Wall Handstand Push-ups

Intermediate

Full HSPU with wall support. Focus on controlled negatives and full range of motion.

Est. time: 3–6 months

3

Freestanding HSPU

Advanced

No wall. Full pressing strength combined with balance mastery. A true test of skill.

Est. time: 6–12 months

4

Deficit HSPU

Elite

Extended range of motion on parallettes. Maximum shoulder strength and control.

Est. time: 12+ months
