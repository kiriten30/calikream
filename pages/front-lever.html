Front Lever.

Horizontal pulling perfection. The front lever builds extraordinary lat strength and iron-core stability through progressive holds.

About This Skill

What is the Front Lever?

The front lever is a static hold performed hanging from a bar with the body held perfectly horizontal, face-up. It demands incredible pulling strength and core tension to maintain the position against gravity.

The movement primarily targets the latissimus dorsi, core muscles, and biceps. It's one of the most respected skills in gymnastics and calisthenics.

Progression

Your Front Lever Ladder.

From tuck to full — master horizontal pulling strength.

1

Tuck Front Lever

Beginner

Knees tucked to chest while hanging horizontal. Build the pulling strength foundation.

Est. time: 2–4 months

2

Advanced Tuck

Intermediate

Flatten the back by opening the hips while keeping knees bent. Significantly harder lever arm.

Est. time: 3–6 months

3

Straddle Front Lever

Advanced

Legs extended and spread wide. Massive increase in difficulty and lat demand.

Est. time: 6–12 months

4

Full Front Lever

Elite

Legs together, body perfectly horizontal. The ultimate pulling hold — requires elite-level back and core strength.

Est. time: 12–24+ months
