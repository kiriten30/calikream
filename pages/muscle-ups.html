Skill Guide

Muscle-ups.

The ultimate compound movement. Pulling yourself above the bar combines explosive strength with precise technique in one fluid motion.

About This Skill

What is a Muscle-up?

The muscle-up is a dynamic movement that combines an explosive pull-up with a dip transition to finish above the bar. It's a benchmark skill in calisthenics — the moment you conquer the bar instead of just hanging from it.

This movement is a full-body exercise demanding coordination between pulling muscles (lats, biceps, forearms) and pushing muscles (chest, triceps, shoulders), making it the ultimate upper-body compound movement.

Progression

Your Muscle-up Ladder.

From false grip to ring muscle-ups — build explosive pulling power.

1

False Grip Training

Beginner

Master the false grip on bar and rings. This wrist position is essential for the transition phase of the muscle-up.

Est. time: 1–3 months

2

Kipping Muscle-up

Intermediate

Use hip drive and swing momentum to clear the bar. Learn the transition movement pattern.

Est. time: 2–6 months

3

Strict Muscle-up

Advanced

No kip, pure pulling strength. The gold standard of muscle-ups — controlled explosive power from dead hang.

Est. time: 6–12 months

4

Ring Muscle-up

Elite

Muscle-up on gymnastics rings. Unstable surface demands peak strength, stability, and coordination.

Est. time: 12+ months
